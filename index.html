Dual-use In-Space Mobility Space Logistics
Impulse Space raised $500M in Series D funding, valuing the company at $4.26 billion. Founded by Tom Mueller (SpaceX's first propulsion engineer), the company builds "space tugs" - spacecraft that move satellites and payloads between orbits. The round was led by 137 Ventures and Banner VC, with participation from Founders Fund, Lux Capital, and Linse Capital. Total funding now exceeds $1 billion since founding in 2021. The company is building full-spectrum space mobility infrastructure serving both commercial operators and US government/DoD customers.

Dual-use Satellite Manufacturing Platform / Bus
Los Angeles-based Apex Space raised over $200M in a growth round led by Glade Brook Capital Partners and Washington Harbour Partners, nearly doubling its valuation to $2.3 billion. The four-year-old company manufactures satellite buses - the structural and operational core platform that handles power, communications and navigation while customers attach mission-specific payloads. Apex serves commercial firms and government/defence customers, positioning as the go-to platform for rapid satellite deployment at scale for both markets.

NewOrbit 🇬🇧
UK (Reading)  |  VLEO Satellites
$18.5M
Series A
VLEO Earth Observation UK
Reading-based NewOrbit closed an oversubscribed $18.5M (EUR 16M) Series A to commercialise very low Earth orbit (VLEO) at 200-300km altitude - offering 20x lower cost than conventional satellites alongside dramatically higher image resolution and faster data speeds. The round was led by Voyager Ventures with participation from Atlantic.vc, Lifeline Ventures, LGF and notable angels including former NVIDIA Chief Scientist David Kirk. Funds will build the NEO Production Complex (opening 2027) and launch the first commercial VLEO satellite in 2028. The oversubscription signals strong investor confidence in the VLEO opportunity.
Space-Comm Outreach Angle
NewOrbit is a UK homegrown success story entering their highest-visibility growth phase - with the NEO Production Complex opening in 2027. Exhibiting at Space-Comm Expo Europe in February 2027 puts them in front of UK government, MOD and international agency buyers at the perfect moment. A UK-flag presence on the show floor is a natural fit and a ready media hook - the kind of story that drives coverage for both the company and the show.
