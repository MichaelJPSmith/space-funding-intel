Impulse Space, founded by SpaceX Merlin engine designer Tom Mueller, closed a $500M Series D led by 137 Ventures and Banner VC - bringing total funding past $1 billion. The company builds Mira maneuvering spacecraft and the Helios kick stage for GEO rideshare, and is aggressively scaling with 131 open roles. Notably, Impulse was named an Anduril collaborator on a US Space Force contract to prototype space-based interceptors - giving it clear dual-use credentials. This was the second-largest funding round globally in the week ending 5 June.
Space-Comm Outreach Angle
Impulse is building the orbital mobility layer everyone else depends on and needs European satellite operators, government primes, and payload customers. Space-Comm Expo Europe 2027 (24-25 Feb, ExCeL London) is the room where those conversations happen. Lead with their Helios GEO rideshare programme and the European launch market opportunity.

Apex, the four-year-old Los Angeles satellite bus manufacturer, raised over $200M led by Glade Brook Capital Partners and Washington Harbour Partners - nearly doubling its valuation to $2.3 billion and pushing total funding above $718M. The company builds spacecraft platforms for both commercial Earth-imaging customers and US government defence programmes, including hardware tied to the Golden Dome missile defence shield. Headcount has doubled to 350+ in the past year and Apex is expanding its Factory One LA campus by 2,800 sqm to meet demand.
Space-Comm Outreach Angle
Apex is scaling manufacturing rapidly and needs European supply chain partners, launch options, and government customers. With UK and European defence procurement accelerating, Space-Comm 2027 is where those conversations happen. Lead with the defence angle - Golden Dome visibility makes them a highly credible exhibitor brand for the government-focused audience at ExCeL.

Unastella 🇰🇷
South Korea  |  Launch vehicles
$24M
Series B
Launch vehiclesSmall satellite launch
Seoul-based Unastella closed a $24M Series B led by Altos Ventures, with Korea Development Bank, Strong Ventures, and Hana Ventures also participating - bringing total funding to $44M. The 22-person startup launched its UNA EXPRESS-I rocket from South Korean soil in May 2025. Founded by former KARI and German Aerospace Center engineer Jae Park, the company uses kerosene/LOX propulsion with electric motor pumps - trading payload capacity for faster time to market. UNA EXPRESS-II is targeted for 2027, and Korea's national space agency has already flown components on the first vehicle.
Space-Comm Outreach Angle
Unastella is actively building European relationships - the founder spent years at the German Aerospace Center and they need European payload customers and government contacts ahead of their 2027 commercial launch campaign. Space-Comm is a natural first European show. Pitch as entry into the UK and EU small satellite launch procurement ecosystem.
